Urban garden in Fredhäll benefit People and bees

The pirates of Mälaren

The Pirates of Mälaren the areas in Fredhäll and Kristineberg were developed in the 1930s and 40s, around forty blocks were named after famous works in Swedish literature. "Kvarteret Mälarpirater" is one of these. The novel "Mälarpirater" was written in 1911 by Sigfrid Siwertz. The garden of the Mälaren Pirates is located along Snoilskyvägen, and many passersby admire the lush garden each season.

For People

Urban gardening offers a unique opportunity to transform your living space into a vibrant oasis while also making a positive impact on the environment. By engaging in urban gardening, you not only get to enjoy the beauty of lush greenery and colorful blooms right at your doorstep, but you also contribute to creating a healthier and more sustainable urban environment. Whether you have a small balcony, a rooftop, or just a tiny corner in your apartment, urban gardening allows you to exercise your creativity, reduce your carbon footprint, and enjoy the therapeutic benefits of nurturing plants. Join the urban gardening movement today and cultivate your own piece of green paradise in the heart of the city.

For bees

Urban gardening plays a crucial role in supporting the declining populations of essential pollinators like bees and butterflies. By creating green spaces in urban environments, we provide much-needed sources of food, shelter, and habitat for these vital insects. By cultivating a diverse range of plants, especially native species rich in nectar and pollen, urban gardens become havens for bees and butterflies to thrive. Through urban gardening practices, we not only enhance the beauty of our cities but also contribute to the conservation of biodiversity and the sustainability of our ecosystems. Join the movement today and be a guardian of these essential pollinators through urban gardening.
